Things you should know before signing a car lease

Publication date: May 10, 2025

Leasing a car may seem simple at first, but small details in the rental agreement can significantly impact your experience and even cost you extra money without you even realizing it. Therefore, it's important to be aware of some key points before signing the contract.

1. Understand the type of insurance offered:

Does the insurance cover accident and theft? Is there a deductible? Check what the insurance covers and what it does not, and don't hesitate to request additional coverage if necessary.

2. Check fuel policy:

Do you have to return your vehicle with a full tank? Some companies charge high fees if you return your vehicle with a low tank. Read the relevant clause carefully.

3. Daily mileage limits:

Some contracts impose a daily mileage limit and charge additional fees if you exceed it. Make sure the limit is sufficient for your travel plan.

4. Special conditions for delay:

How late are you allowed to deliver? What are the late fees? Sometimes the fee doubles after the first hour of delay.

5. Check the condition of the car before signing:

Conduct a thorough inspection of the vehicle before receiving it, and note any scratches or damage in the contract, preferably documenting the condition with photos. This protects you from the cost of damage you didn't cause.

6. Cancellation Policy:

If I have to cancel my reservation, will I receive a refund? What is the time period for cancellation without fees?

7. Driver age and license requirements:

Some companies charge additional fees for drivers under 25 or over 65. You should also ensure that your license is valid and recognized.

8. Additional undisclosed fees:

Read the bill carefully. Is there a cleaning fee? A service fee? A delivery fee to a different location? Make sure you understand all costs before signing.

Conclusion

Reading your car lease contract carefully will save you a lot of confusion and surprise fees later on. Don't hesitate to ask the rental agent questions if you don't understand a particular clause.
